How it works

Recrete uses a machine learning model trained on 1,030 laboratory concrete mix samples to predict compressive strength and optimize mix proportions.

ASTM compliance is enforced through hard-coded constraint tables — not inferred by ML. The system then minimizes embodied carbon while meeting all performance and regulatory requirements.

Regional supplier availability is factored into optimization to minimize transport emissions.

Engineering rationale
⚡ AI-assisted · qualitative only · not compliance

The optimizer selected a ternary blend replacing 51% of OPC with GGBS (36%) and Class C fly ash (14%). At W2 exposure for a bridge deck, ACI 318-19 §19.3.2 requires w/cm ≤ 0.45 and f'c ≥ 28 MPa — both satisfied with margin.

GGBS at 36% replacement improves long-term durability by refining pore structure and reducing chloride permeability, which is critical for deicing salt exposure. The superplasticizer dose (7.2 kg/m³) maintains workability at a low w/cm of 0.42 without increasing water content.

The 60 kg/m³ fly ash contribution reduces heat of hydration — beneficial for a deck slab — while further lowering carbon. Note: fly ash at this dose may extend setting time by 30–60 min in cool weather. Air entrainment should be specified separately per ACI 318 §26.4.3 for freeze-thaw resistance.
